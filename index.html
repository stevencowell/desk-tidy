Main Theory Content

Week 1: Safety, Introduction to Materials & Project Brief

Workshop Safety: In the workshop, safety is the top priority. Everyone must learn and follow basic safety rules to prevent accidents. Always listen carefully to your teacher’s instructions and never use any tool or machine without permission and proper training. Do not run or play around in the workshop – move calmly and stay focused on what you are doing. If you are unsure how to use something, always ask for help.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Wear your safety gear every time. Safety glasses protect your eyes from flying wood chips and dust when cutting or sanding. An apron (or workshop coat) protects your clothes and keeps loose clothing secure so it doesn’t get caught in tools. If you have long hair, tie it back, and remove any loose jewellery or ties. Wear closed, sturdy shoes to protect your feet. Wearing the right PPE helps keep you safe while you work.

Safe Tool Use and Behavior: Always use tools for their intended purpose and in the correct way. Keep both hands behind the cutting edge of tools. For example, when using a saw or chisel, your free hand should be well away from the blade path. Only one person should use a tool or machine at a time. Keep your work area tidy and return tools after use.

Introduction to Materials: We will mainly use wood. Softwoods like pine come from fast-growing coniferous trees, while hardwoods like oak are from slower-growing deciduous trees. Manufactured boards such as plywood or MDF are man-made sheets useful for stability.

Project Brief – Desk Tidy: Design and build a wooden desk tidy to hold stationery. Think about what items you want it to hold and how to make it practical and good-looking. Safety always comes first.

Week 2: Tools and Equipment – Using Hand Tools Safely

Working on a project involves many hand tools. Know each tool and use it correctly. Always check tools are in good condition and secure your workpiece with a clamp or vice before cutting or drilling.

Common tools include rulers, pencils, try squares, tenon saws, bench hooks, coping saws, clamps or vices, files, sandpaper, drills, hammers, screwdrivers, and chisels with mallets. Maintain tools and tell your teacher if one is damaged.

Week 3: Materials – Timber Types and Sustainability

Wood types vary. Softwoods like pine grow quickly and are easier to cut. Hardwoods like oak grow slower and are stronger and often more expensive. Manufactured boards such as plywood, MDF, and chipboard are made from layers or fibers bonded together.

Sustainable use means sourcing timber responsibly (look for FSC certification) and minimizing waste. Wood is renewable and biodegradable, making it environmentally friendly when managed well.

Week 5: Measuring and Marking Out

Accurate measurement and marking are vital. Measure from the zero mark, use a sharp pencil, mark the waste side, and double-check before cutting. Use a try square for right angles. Remember: measure twice, cut once.
