Week 1: Advanced Content

Safety in a workshop isn’t just a classroom rule – it’s a professional requirement known as Work Health and Safety (WHS). Professionals follow strict safety protocols. Loud machinery may require ear protection and fine wood dust often means wearing a mask or using special ventilation. Around 2,000 U.S. workers suffer an eye injury each day, and most could be prevented with proper protection. This shows why wearing safety goggles is so important. In advanced workshops you may also see safety signs reminding everyone to wear PPE and be cautious.

Case Study: In one woodworking shop, a craftsman skipped wearing safety glasses for a quick cut. A small piece of wood flew up and permanently injured his eye. Since then, that shop enforces a rule that no one cuts anything without eyewear, even for a second.

Advanced Activities – Week 1

  1. Identify at least three potential hazards in a wood workshop and explain how you would reduce the risk for each.

  2. How do you think safety in a school workshop compares to safety in a big factory woodworking shop? What might be the same and what might be different?

  3. Explain how personal protective equipment requirements might differ for a complex woodworking job compared to a simple classroom task.

Week 2: Advanced Content

This week covers advanced layout tools like marking gauges and sliding bevels for precise measurement of complex shapes.

Advanced Activities – Week 2

  1. Explain how a marking gauge improves accuracy when laying out parallel lines.

  2. When would you use a sliding bevel instead of a combination square?

  3. Provide a detailed example of how you would use both a marking gauge and a sliding bevel to mark out an angled cut.

Week 3: Advanced Content

We explore joinery options such as housing joints and rebates to create stronger connections.

Advanced Activities – Week 3

  1. Describe a situation where a housing joint is preferred over a butt joint.

  2. List two benefits of using a router to cut rebates.

  3. Describe the steps to create a rebate using a router, including important safety precautions.

Week 4: Advanced Content

Students refine their designs using simple prototypes and 3D visualisation tools.

Advanced Activities – Week 4

  1. How can a cardboard mock-up reveal issues in your design?

  2. Name one digital tool that could help visualise the desk tidy in 3D.

  3. Write a brief plan outlining how you would test and refine your design using both physical prototypes and digital 3D models.

Week 5: Advanced Content

This week we look at advanced measuring and marking techniques to ensure every component fits perfectly. Consider how precise jigs and templates help maintain consistency across multiple pieces.

Advanced Activities – Week 5

  1. Describe how you would create a jig to mark out identical side panels quickly and accurately.

  2. List two common causes of measurement error when transferring dimensions from plans to timber and how to avoid them.

  3. Digital calipers and laser measures are becoming popular in workshops. Discuss one advantage and one limitation of these tools compared with traditional rulers and squares.

  4. Explain why accuracy is critical when using jigs and describe a method to verify that your jig produces consistent parts.

Week 6: Advanced Content

Focus on sanding and surface preparation including grain filling for open-pore timbers.

Advanced Activities – Week 6

  1. Why should you sand with progressively finer grits before finishing?

  2. What does a grain filler do when finishing timber?

  3. Detail the step-by-step sanding and grain filling process you would follow before applying a finish.

Week 7: Advanced Content

Different finishing products are compared, including oils, varnishes and waxes.

Advanced Activities – Week 7

  1. Which finish would provide the most durable surface for a frequently handled desk tidy?

  2. List a safety precaution to follow when applying oil-based finishes.

  3. Describe how you would decide between using an oil finish and a varnish for the desk tidy, considering appearance and durability.

Investigate hardware options such as hinges or magnets to add features to the project.

Advanced Activities – Week 8

This week focuses on user testing and evaluating feedback to refine the design.

The project wraps up with cost calculations and reflection on skills gained.
